Sara Duterte's Isang Kaibigan: An Account of Misallocated Funds and Editorial Errors Sparks Controversy

Jonas Elcid David

3 min read • August 31, 2024
Vice President Sara Duterte holding her self-authorized book, “Isang Kaibigan”

In a turn of events, a national debate has centered around Vice President Sara Duterte's children's book project called "Isang Kaibigan." The controversy is the result of a mix of questionable fundraising requests and humiliating editing errors, which cloud the initiative's good intentions and raise important issues with accountability and government in the Philippines.

Duterte's request for an astounding ₱10 million in government financing to publish and distribute Isang Kaibigan, a book meant to educate Filipinos, especially kids, on the principles of community and friendship, is at the center of the issue. Several societal groups have sharply criticized this huge amount, with many questioning the rationality of such an allocation in a nation where many public schools struggle with outdated texts, limited facilities, and underpaid teachers.

Vice President Sara Duterte, who was also the Education secretary at the time, launched the book “Isang Kaibigan” at Esteban Abada Elementary School on November 21, 2023.

The controversy is the result of a mix of questionable fundraising requests and humiliating editing errors, which cloud the initiative's good intentions and raise important issues with accountability and government in the Philippines.

Some argue that addressing these urgent problems in the field of education would have been a better use of the money. Juan Dela Cruz, an education policy expert at the University of the Philippines, said, "Allocating such a large sum to a single book project seems misguided at best in a nation where many students still lack basic educational resources" (ABS-CBN News, 2024)​.

The dispute intensified as readers found numerous mistakes in Isang Kaibigan's pages. The book's content has come under fire for being below the standards required of an instructional tool, especially one that is financed by public funds, with apparent grammatical errors and factual inaccuracies. The credibility of the book has been damaged by these mistakes, which have also brought up major questions regarding the quality control procedures used or not used during the book's preparation and publication.

Image from ANC News

The Isang Kaibigan incident has sparked a rapid and largely unfavorable public response. Social media users have expressed their dissatisfaction and disappointment in large quantities, with the hashtag #IsangKaibigan becoming popular as a result. Many see the scenario as a sign of a larger trend in which public funds are directed toward initiatives that advance the interests of the powerful over the interests of the general public.

The irony that a book meant to impart moral lessons about friendship and integrity is currently entangled in a dispute over financial and editorial mismanagement. Particularly in the education sector, this incident has further damaged public confidence in the government's capacity to handle public finances appropriately.

The controversy has reignited conversations about the need for more robust checks and balances in the distribution and utilization of public funds. More accountability and transparency are demanded by opposition politicians, who have asked for a probe into the funding request and the book's development process.

The Isang Kaibigan dispute is a reminder of the difficulties that the Philippine government is facing. It draws attention to the fine line that must be drawn between creative educational endeavors and responsible financial management, as well as the necessity of upholding high standards in programs that receive public funding.

The future of Isang Kaibigan is still up in the air while the argument rages on. What is certain, though, is that the public accountability system and the future of government-funded projects in the Philippines will probably be affected negatively by this dispute for some time. Civil society organizations and educational institutions have voiced their worries, extending the public outrage beyond social media channels. It functions as a warning story, highlighting the necessity of stricter scrutiny, open decision-making, and a revitalized dedication to advancing the public interest.

As the Philippines deals with the aftermath of this incident, there is still hope that it will act as a spark for improvement, bringing in a new era of increased responsibility and more prudent use of public funds to support the country's educational objectives.
